Privacy and Terms

Privacy Policy
Tourism New Brunswick respects the privacy of individuals who visit www.TourismNewBrunswick.ca. Any personal information provided to us through this website and affiliated channels is collected, used and disclosed in accordance with the New Brunswick Right to Information and Protection of Privacy Act and other applicable legislation. The information collected is used to help Tourism New Brunswick understand how visitors are using our site(s) and to continuously improve user experience. This policy outlines the information which Tourism New Brunswick collects from you and how that information is used.

Personally Identifiable Information
Personally Identifiable Information is information that can be used to identify or locate a specific individual. It could include an individual’s name, street address, email address or phone number. Personally Identifiable Information will not be collected unless you voluntarily send an email message, complete an online form or provide personal information for a contest or survey.

Other information collected from visitors to the Tourism New Brunswick website include:

  • Internet Protocol (IP) address from computers that access the website
  • Date and time the website is accessed
  • Pages visited on the website
  • URL of the resource that referred the visitor to the website
  • Content downloaded from the website

Google
Tourismnewbrunswick.ca uses a variety of Google Applications, including Google Analytics and Google Maps, to provide users with relevant information and to enhance their online experience. Through these applications, Google collects users’ IP addresses. You are offered choices when asked for personal information, such as the ability to decline providing the information and/or refuse cookies in your browser. However, this may prevent personalized services from being provided and you may not be able to take full advantage of all the features available. 

To learn more about how Google collects and stores IP address, please see Google's Privacy Centre. Further information on Google Applications and Google’s policies on the protection of personal information, can be found through Google's Privacy FAQ.

Third Party Advertising
Cookies are small pieces of data that websites place in computers to track what information is viewed on the site and assist visitors in returning to specific pages. Tourism New Brunswick uses cookies to track visitor activities and personalize information for returning guests. Tourism New Brunswick uses advertising partners (such as Google and Facebook) to provide online visitors with relevant advertisements.  These third party advertisers may use cookies to improve targeted advertising.  If you do not wish to receive such advertisement you can opt out-out at the Digital Advertisement Alliance of Canada opt-out page.

Electronic Newsletters
We offer free email newsletters to those who voluntarily subscribe. You will only receive an email newsletter if you have signed up or opted-in, either on this website or an affiliate channel of Tourism New Brunswick. We do not share our mailing lists with third parties.

Surveys
Tourism New Brunswick periodically conducts online surveys in order to gather general information about visitors to the website. Participation in these surveys is voluntary and no information is collected other than that which is knowingly supplied. Personally Identifiable Information provided by the participant will not be shared.

Contests
Tourism New Brunswick occasionally runs contests on this website and/or affiliate channels which online visitors can optionally enter. Entrants may be required to submit varying degrees of personal information. Required information and how it will be used will be disclosed in the contest Rules and Regulations. Some elements of contest winner’s Personally Identifiable Information, such as name and likeness, may be used for Tourism New Brunswick marketing purposes.

Third-Party Social Media
Tourism New Brunswick maintains official pages or accounts on several social media websites in order to better engage with visitors and promote awareness of our activities, events and information. Tourism New Brunswick’s use of social media serves as an extension of its presence on the web. Social media account(s) are public and users who choose to interact with us via social media should read the terms of service and privacy policies of these third-party service providers and those of any applications you use to access them. Social media websites used by Tourism New Brunswick include, but are not limited to, Facebook, Twitter, YouTube, Pinterest, Storify, Instagram and TripAdvisor. Personal information that you provide to Tourism New Brunswick via social media account(s) is collected to share and re-post photos and videos, as well as to capture conversations (e.g. questions and answers, comments, “likes”, retweets, posts) between you and Tourism New Brunswick. It may be used to respond to inquiries, or for marketing, statistical, evaluation and reporting purposes. Comments that violate Tourism New Brunswick rules of engagement will be deleted.

Tourism New Brunswick may embed social media content from the third parties listed above. Tourism New Brunswick does not endorse and is not responsible for the content of external sites.  Links may open in a new window.

Accuracy of Information
Tourism New Brunswick uses reasonable efforts to ensure all information on the Tourism New Brunswick website and affiliated channels is accurate and up-to-date. Such accuracy cannot be guaranteed, however, and your reliance of information provided on this site is at your own risk. All content, including this policy, is subject to change without notice.

Legal Authority
The information collected by Tourism New Brunswick is collected under the authority of section 2 of the Tourism Development Act, 2008 and is required for the purposes of programs and activities of Tourism New Brunswick.
